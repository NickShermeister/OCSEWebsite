It's time to re-imagine engineer hiring.

Open Cross-School Exhibitions (OCSE) is here to reinvent the hiring process for engineers, so companies can see students' finished work and so students are able to network both with companies and students at other colleges.

LEARN MORE

Students have really cool projects.

You know it, we know it, and it's time to show it.

    As personal projects and project classes become pervasive in upper education, students are starting to have really cool projects that are hard to show to companies through two lines on a resume. What we want is to celebrate students’ hard work and accomplishments while giving companies the opportunity to meet and see these projects in-action. Through this need, the Open Cross-School Exhibition was born.

"first event will be at the conclusion of January 2020"

Our first event will be at the conclusion of January 2020 in Boston. We are partnering with Olin College, Harvard University, Boston University, and Tufts University to invite students from these colleges and more to come into Boston and show off their recent accomplishments and connect with professors, employers, and each other. We are also inviting companies from the greater Boston area and beyond to check out the young tech talent in action.

Companies are scrambling to hire new tech talent in the current competitive marketplace, and too often they hire someone who appears to be competent on paper but upon arrival cannot even open a command terminal. With an average hiring cost of $50,000 for an engineer, getting a bad hire can be disastrous.

On the other hand, the internship/job market for undergraduate engineers is competitive and students are motivated to show their potential. At the same time, projects are increasingly becoming a staple in engineering education, but at the end of the semester they are left on a shelf or thrown out, producing no more value after the pain and learning required to build them.

There are many current events that attempt to provide value to one or both parties, but each has its own flaws: 

  • CAREER FAIR - Career fair are a great place to hire, but only so much can be conveyed on a resume or during a elevator pitch.
  • PROFESSIONAL CONFERENCES - Professional conferences fill this need but are more intended for graduate students and researchers.
  • HACKATHONS - Hackathons are an okay place to network and connect with employers, but work displayed is performed hastily, they require an entire weekend, and there is rarely set time for networking. 
  • END OF YEAR DEMOS -  End-of-year demos or exhibitions showcase high quality undergraduate work but are smaller and more specific, and usually do not attract many companies
Our conferences will follow a protocol similar to that of poster sessions at larger research conferences, where we allow students as either individuals or teams to present projects that they have worked on for the previous semester. Conferences will take place at the beginning of the fall and spring semesters, targeting undergraduate students.
